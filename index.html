About Me

In May of 2023, I will graduate from The Ohio State University with a B.S. in Computer Science and Engineering. I plan to specialize in both Artificial Intelligence and Database Systems/Data Analytics, while also minoring in Studio Art and Mathematics. To learn more about my computer science experience, please visit Programming and Documentation and my resume. Examples of my artwork can be found both in the art and design galleries.

Throughout high school, my experience with the iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machines) team synthetic biology research project increased my interest in research.

While still in high school, I held two synthetic biology research internships in the 711th Human Performance Wing as part of the Air Force Research Laboratories (AFRL) iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machines) team. Although much of my work was in the wet lab, I sought opportunities to focus on mathematical modeling, data analytics, and website development. I led a collaboration of more than thirty iGEM teams to develop an in-depth written guide, a YouTube series, and sample HTML/CSS code to document how to create the required iGEM team website.

In October 2018, I applied to a job shadow at Wright Patterson Air Force Base. The research I was exposed to inspired me to report on the current research of aircraft detection using Convolutional Neural Networks for my Advanced Placement Computer Science course in high school. The following summer I returned to where I had job shadowed as a Wright Scholar intern where I designed and programmed a Graphical User Interphase for database filtering.

Professional Goals

My experiences this far have solidified my interest in becoming an Artificial Intelligence research computer scientist. The areas that I saw during an AFRL job shadow have led me to consider computer vision/image processing to be my preferred area, although I cannot be certain until I have experience this area. The power to use machine learning to better understand data in this way is inspiring.

In my undergraduate career, I hope to become involved with computer science research. After I earn my Bachelor's Degree, I will continue my education by going to graduate school to get a Master's Degree in Computer Science. Currently, I am only planning on pursuing a master’s degree, but I have realized that I may want to go deeper into research or begin teaching, so I have considered that a PhD may be a good option later on.
