Airplane Collection

In 2018, I completed this collection to use as part of this website's design. I took the original photos at the United States Air Force Museum (Dayton, OH), then digitally manipulated them in Adobe Photoshop. I entered this collection in the Scholastic Art Competition, where one of the planes won an Honorable Mention. If you click on each photo, you can see that each title is a different quote from one of the Wright Brothers - the two Daytonians who invented the airplane. Since their quotes are from the time that they were creating the first plane, they create an interesting contrast with the subject matter.

"No flying machine will ever fly from New York to Paris." -Orville Wright

"We tossed a coin to decide who should have the first trial. Wilbur won." -Orville Wright

"I confess that in 1901, I said to my brother Orville that man would not fly for 50 years." -Wilbur Wright

"The airplane stays up because it doesn't have the time to fall." -Orville Wright

"We had had a number of experiences in which we had landed upon one wing." -Orville Wright

"It is possible to fly without motors, but not without knowledge and skill." -Wilbur Wright

"If birds can glide for long periods of time, then...why can't I?" -Orville Wright

"We had taken up aeronautics merely as a sport." -Orville Wright

Space Race Infographic

One of the assignments in my second-year writing course was to design an infographic about whatever we wanted. I've always been interested in space, so I chose to research the events that occurred during the Space Race to get to the moon. I used Adobe Illustrator to draw each of the elements and design the entire infographic.
